DBC's Learning Approach

06/11/14

Objectives: Consider DBC's culture and learning approach, and predict how you will react to this approach.

I would summarize Shereef's fireside chat as discussing a culture of experimentation and construction. He emphasizes tbe newness of the DBC program structure, which relates to a sense of adventure shared among both students and staff.

I think this approach should work for me. I always work better and learn more when I feel needed, so I like the emphasis that students contribute to the effectiveness of the program as a whole (through feedback to each other, suggestions to staff, etc.).

I also like that the culture is open to change - this seems to reflect an ability to take reasonable risks and a dedicated interest in providing the right support system to take risks. I think for much of my life, I've spent more time with people who have an adequate support system but remain unwilling to take risks.

Perhaps most importantly, the human component of DBC's culture appeals to me and was the main reason that pushed me to take this opportunity. Empathy is something that I consider often (how I can be more empathetic and how I can help others be more empathetic), so I'm looking forward to meeting and learning from people who share these values.

I am nervous to experience what the time commitment will feel like. I've always had many activities that I like to spend time on regularly, so I'll likely feel a little sad and burned out if I don't have time to do other things. Shereef's answer to the question addressing DBC's hours alleviated this nervousness somewhat, but it will be hard to judge until I get there.
